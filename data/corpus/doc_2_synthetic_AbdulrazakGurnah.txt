Abdulrazak Gurnah is a Tanzanian-born novelist who won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2021. His novels explore themes of exile, belonging, and cultural identity, often set in East Africa and among diasporic communities. Works like 'Paradise', 'The By-pass', and 'Dottie' examine the impact of colonialism and its aftermath. Gurnah's narrative style blends historical fiction with intimate character studies, revealing how individuals navigate identity in post-colonial contexts. His recognition by the Nobel committee brought international attention to African literature and writers from the East African region.
