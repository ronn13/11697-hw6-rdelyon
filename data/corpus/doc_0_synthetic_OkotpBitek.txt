Okot p'Bitek was a prominent Ugandan poet, novelist, and political satirist. Born in 1931, he is best known for his poem 'Song of Lawino', a powerful critique of colonial education and cultural imperialism in Uganda. His work celebrates African cultural pride and challenges Western impositions on African society. p'Bitek's literary style combined traditional Acholi oral tradition with contemporary themes, making his works accessible to both educated and common audiences. He wrote in English and Acholi, demonstrating linguistic versatility. His contributions to African literature remain influential in postcolonial studies.
