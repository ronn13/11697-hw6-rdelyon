Ngugi wa Thiong'o is a Kenyan author and playwright renowned for his novels exploring colonialism, independence, and social justice. His major works include 'The River Between', 'A Grain of Wheat', and 'Petals of Blood'. Ngugi writes in both English and Gikuyu, advocating for African languages in literature. His commitment to decolonizing the mind through writing has made him a seminal figure in African literature. He has faced political persecution for his activism and writing. Ngugi's work examines the complexities of postcolonial Kenya and the struggle for liberation.
